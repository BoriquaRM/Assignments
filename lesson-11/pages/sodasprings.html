"Bravado"
by David Olsen

The arena was set, the lights dimmed, and both champions sat… but only one was truly ready for what came next.

The surrounding crowd fell silent. No murmur was heard, not even the whistling wind dared break the focus.

A fist clenched around the instrument of choice, one which had claimed many already. Perhaps today, another.

The other sat, defenseless, yet feigning ignorance at the danger presented.

The fist lifted, pointing a finger. The finger moved rapidly, then returned as a fist again.

The battle… had begun.

*ring, ring*

“Hello?”

“Yes, yes, hello?”

“Can I help you?”

“Ah, yes sir, hello, I am with Computer Customer Care, how are you doing today?”

“Fine, I guess. What’s this about?”

“Oh sir, our records indicate that your computer has been infected with computer viruses. This is most unfortunate, as it can slow down your system, delete files and be a very real problem. I’m calling to help you delete these awful viruses.”

Hmph, sure you are.

There are moments of my life when I am compelled to admit stupidity, most of them being in my teen years, yet I am no stranger to bullies, punks or even the whipper-snapping hooligan. In this case, it took the form of a Middle-Eastern scammer who has now called for the third time to tell me about viruses that I don’t have.

It’s especially bad when he has to ask for my name. He’s monitoring my computer, but doesn’t know who I am. Right.

Like the other two times before, I was content on hanging up without further argument, but I paused, caught in a mental tug-of-war. I’d spent the greater portion of my young life being picked on, pushed around, taken advantage of. Like a wounded puppy that was kicked one time too many, I made a decision.

This time… I would have some fun.

“Oh, sorry, I just dropped something, didn’t catch all of that. Did you say something about getting rid of viruses?”

“Oh yes, sir, we are aware that you’re infected with many, many of them, and it should be taken care of right away.”

“Oh, finally! I’ve been trying to get someone to look at them for a while now. See, I just got back from an overseas trip, and I’ve had problems ever since.”

“Well then, sir, I’m happy to help, can you…”

“So it started off as a rash. Just red and blotchy, you know? Really itchy.”

“I can… wait, what?”

“But then it got worse the more I scratched it, which yeah, they tell you not to do, but that topical cream wasn’t working, and it started going up my…”

“Sir? Sir! I am sorry, no, but I cannot help you with those kinds of viruses.”

“Oh, this isn’t the dermatologist?”

“No sir, this is Computer Service Care, for the removal of computer viruses.”

“Oh. Oh! Wow, yeah, I guess you didn’t need to know any of that, huh?”

“No sir, completely unnecessary.”

“Alright, well, what do you need me to do?”

“I need you to go to your desktop computer.”

“Oh, yeah, of course, but it’s in my bedroom.”

“Excellent, go ahead and go to it.”

“It’s kind of old though, still has Windows 95. Is that a problem?”

“No, not a problem, sir, go ahead and power it on.”

“Oh, umm, I don’t actually have a power cord for it, it’s just been sitting in my closet for a few years. But you know what, I can go run to Best Buy real quick and get a cord. Do you mind waiting on the phone for about 30 minutes while I get it?”

“No, sir, no, this is not needed… do you have a laptop instead?”

“Oh yeah, of course!”

“Okay, that will work just fine, please go find your laptop.”

“Sure thing.” (whistling) “Pepper!”

“… uh, sir? What are you…?”

“Pep… oh there you are, Peps, how you doin’ buddy? Who’s a gooood booooy?”

“Sir, what… what is going on?”

“You asked for my lapdog. This little guy is a Pomeranian, and he’s sooo cuuuute, yes you aaare. Kisses!”

“Okay, sir? Sir! Please, I just need you to turn on your laptop.”

“… kinda sounds like a job for a girl dog, don’t you think?”

*click*

Every day is a good day for a little bravado.

THE END
